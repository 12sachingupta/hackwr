Why is it important to check website safety?

The internet is full of scam websites that criminals use to steal your money and personal details. While stealing money is obviously profitable for criminals, they can also profit from your personal details. For example, they can sell them to other criminals, or use them for fraud and identity theft.While losing money is bad, stolen personal details can be used even years later for example to take loans in your name. And not only is fixing the issue costly, but it can also take a lot of time and cause immense stress. Checking website safety is an effortless step in preventing criminals from stealing your money and personal data.

When you scan a URL, this unique tool checks if it’s safe. The results are based on millions of thoroughly analyzed websites. Here are some of the things that are analyzed:

- Technical aspects such as website popularity, how long it has been operational, redirections, backlinks, server information, search engine blocking, SSL certificate, and many others.

Based on the extensive analyses, this website checker displays a score for the shopping sites. The higher the score, the safer the site.

Most legit web shops care about the technical performance of their site and will not likely raise any other red flags either, so they will get rated well. On the other hand, scam websites are often very new. While it is easy to create a website, taking care of all the technical details that this website checker looks into requires time and resources that scammers don’t usually have or want to use. Because of the multi-layered analysis, the score gives an accurate result of website safety. And while a suspicious site may not always be malicious, there are very good reasons to avoid it.

How to tell if a website is legit or a scam?

Scams can be hard to detect, but there are some telltale signs you can check for when deciding if a website is legit or not. If you scan the URL with this a free website checker, but are still unsure whether it’s safe, here are some things you can look for.

- Check the URL. Some scam websites look the same as real trusted stores. However, their web addresses (aka URLs) can include miniscule one-character differences that set them apart from these trusted stores. Check the URL to spot these differences, and any other weird details there might be.
- Look for reviews online. What others have said in social media or review sites can save you a lot of trouble. However, keep in mind that reviews can be fabricated. If reviews are very short and excessively written by generic profile names such as John117, be on your guard.
- Look for contact information. When you check if a shopping website is legit, their contact information can tell a lot. For example, if they do not have any or it is very difficult to find, something is off. Trusted online shopping sites would not hide such information. The same goes for their return policy.
- Beware slashed prices and timed offers. Scam shopping websites lure us to buy as fast as possible. They can offer ludicrous prices and for a very limited time (for example 5 minutes), so that you act before you carefully think it through or check website safety in any way. While trusted online shopping sites can use similar tactics, they are excessively used by scammers.
- Pay with credit card or PayPal. This tip will not help you spot a scam. But if all else fails and you happen to fall for one, it’s a lot easier to get your money back this way than when paying with a bank transfer or other direct payment methods.
